# Rule ID

MSG-020

# Title

Replay safety

# Category

Events and Messaging

# Status

Active

# Intent

Evaluate whether replay or reprocessing can occur without incorrect duplicate effects, unsafe historical interpretation, or uncontrolled inconsistencies.

# Applicability

Apply this rule when messages may be replayed, reprocessed, retained and consumed later, used to rebuild projections, repaired manually, resent operationally, or processed after checkpoints.

Do not apply this rule when replay or reprocessing cannot occur in the reviewed context and retained messages are not used for later processing. Do not presume Event Sourcing.

# Rule Statement

When replay or reprocessing is possible, the architecture must ensure repeated historical processing is safe for state, external effects, contract versions, ordering, checkpoints, and changed business rules within the reviewed context.

# Rationale

Replay can rebuild projections and support recovery, but it can also resend external effects, apply current rules to historical facts, process incomplete sequences, reinterpret old schemas, or overwrite current state with stale events. Replay safety is broader than ordinary idempotency but does not make replay mandatory.

# Evidence Required

Evaluation requires evidence of replay or reprocessing paths, retained messages, projection rebuilds, checkpoints, handler side effects, idempotency controls, ordering assumptions, schema and version handling, historical data interpretation, external dependencies, manual operations, tests, and documentation.

Primary evidence includes replay tools, consumer code, projection logic, side-effect guards, schema transformations, checkpoint storage, sequence handling, reprocessing runbooks, tests using historical messages, logs, and ADRs.

# Evaluation Guidance

Determine what replay means in the reviewed system: projection rebuild, operational resend, manual repair, retained-message catch-up, or full stream replay. Evaluate whether handlers can distinguish live processing from replay when needed and whether historical facts are interpreted with the correct contract and rule context.

Common finding shapes include replay sending customer notifications again, rebuilding projections with current rules that invalidate historical facts, reprocessing without checkpoint safety, partial replay missing prerequisite messages, old contract versions unsupported, non-deterministic consumers producing different results, and manual replay bypassing idempotency.

Remediation may include separating external side effects from rebuild paths, guarding idempotent effects, preserving historical contract interpretation, documenting replay modes, validating sequence completeness, managing checkpoints, and testing reprocessing with retained messages. Do not require Event Sourcing or replay for every system.

Boundaries: `MSG-013` evaluates repeated effect safety in ordinary consumption; `MSG-015` evaluates ordering; `MSG-019` evaluates traceability; this rule evaluates the combined safety of deliberate or operational reprocessing.

# Pass Conditions

Use `Pass` when evidence shows replay or reprocessing paths are safe for relevant state, side effects, schema versions, ordering, checkpoints, historical interpretation, and operational controls.

# Fail Conditions

Use `Fail` when direct evidence shows replay or reprocessing can create duplicate external effects, corrupt state, misinterpret historical facts, skip required sequence, apply incompatible schemas, or bypass controls with real impact.

# Warning Conditions

Use `Warning` when replay safety is partial, undocumented, untested, manually dependent, safe only for some consumers, or unclear around side effects, schema versions, ordering, or checkpoints.

# Not Applicable Conditions

Use `Not Applicable` when replay, retained-message reprocessing, projection rebuild, operational resend, and manual reprocessing are not present or relevant in the reviewed scope.

# Not Enough Evidence Conditions

Use `Not Enough Evidence` when material does not show replay paths, retained messages, handlers, side effects, checkpoints, schema version handling, ordering, historical rules, or operational procedures.

# Severity Guidance

Severity depends on duplicated side effects, state corruption, projection criticality, historical data volume, recovery importance, consumer count, financial or regulatory exposure, and ability to detect and repair replay damage.

# Confidence Guidance

Use `Confirmed` when direct evidence shows replay behavior and safety or violation. Use `Likely` when multiple sources align with incomplete operational or historical detail. Use `Possible` for limited signals. Use `Not Enough Evidence` when replay existence or safety cannot be established. Event storage alone must not produce `Confirmed`.
