# Rule ID

MSG-016

# Title

Retry strategy

# Category

Events and Messaging

# Status

Active

# Intent

Evaluate whether retries for message production, dispatch, or consumption are bounded, observable, and compatible with failure type and system impact.

# Applicability

Apply this rule when producers, dispatchers, brokers, consumers, external calls, acknowledgements, or processing steps can fail transiently and retry behavior exists, is configured, or is expected.

Do not apply this rule when no retryable operation exists in the reviewed scope or when failures are intentionally not retried with documented and acceptable behavior.

# Rule Statement

Retries must be designed so transient failures can be retried without infinite repetition, retry storms, hidden loss, uncontrolled duplicate effects, or unobservable stuck processing.

# Rationale

Retries can improve resilience, but unbounded or misclassified retries can overload dependencies, repeat side effects, hide permanent failures, delay diagnosis, and interfere with acknowledgement, dead-letter, and recovery behavior.

# Evidence Required

Evaluation requires evidence of retry policies, limits, delay or backoff behavior, error classification, acknowledgement interaction, side-effect interaction, logging, metrics, escalation, dead-letter handoff, tests, and operational procedures.

Primary evidence includes producer, dispatcher, and consumer code; configuration; broker policies; resilience libraries; logs; metrics; tests; and ADRs. Specific numeric values are not required by this rule.

# Evaluation Guidance

Evaluate whether retries are appropriate to failure type and impact. Distinguish transient failures from permanent failures, local retries from platform retries, and retrying a message from retrying an external side effect.

Common finding shapes include infinite retry loops, no cap or escalation, retry storms against degraded dependencies, retrying deterministic validation failures, lost context across attempts, ack behavior that conflicts with retry, and no observability for exhausted retries.

Remediation may include limiting attempts, classifying failure types, adding delay/backoff/jitter conceptually, preserving retry context, exposing retry metrics or logs, coordinating with idempotency, and routing exhausted messages to an operational path. Do not prescribe values, libraries, or dead-letter as the whole retry strategy.

Boundaries: `MSG-011` covers publication record resend; `MSG-012` covers delivery semantics; `MSG-013` covers consumer effect safety under repeated processing; `MSG-017` covers dead-letter handling; `MSG-018` covers poison message isolation.

# Pass Conditions

Use `Pass` when evidence shows retries are bounded, observable, appropriate for transient failures, coordinated with acknowledgement and side effects, and have an escalation or stop condition suitable for the reviewed context.

# Fail Conditions

Use `Fail` when direct evidence shows retry behavior creates real risk through infinite loops, retry storms, repeated side effects, hidden permanent failures, lost context, or no stop/escalation path.

# Warning Conditions

Use `Warning` when retry strategy is partial, poorly documented, weakly observable, missing failure classification, or dependent on defaults without enough evidence for a confirmed failure.

# Not Applicable Conditions

Use `Not Applicable` when retryable message failure behavior is outside the reviewed scope or failures are intentionally not retried with acceptable evidenced impact.

# Not Enough Evidence Conditions

Use `Not Enough Evidence` when material does not show retry configuration, failure handling, acknowledgement behavior, side effects, limits, observability, or exhausted-retry path.

# Severity Guidance

Severity depends on duplicate effect risk, dependency saturation, critical flow impact, backlog growth, operational detectability, recovery cost, and whether failures are transient, permanent, or safety-critical.

# Confidence Guidance

Use `Confirmed` when direct evidence shows retry behavior and its impact or adequacy. Use `Likely` when multiple signals align with incomplete operational detail. Use `Possible` for limited signals. Use `Not Enough Evidence` when retry behavior cannot be established. Library presence alone must not produce `Confirmed`.

# Dependencies

MSG-011, MSG-012, MSG-013, MSG-017, MSG-018. These are adjacent responsibilities, not procedural prerequisites.
