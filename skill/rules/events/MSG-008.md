# Rule ID

MSG-008

# Title

Event contract compatibility

# Category

Events and Messaging

# Status

Active

# Intent

Evaluate whether message contract evolution preserves sufficient producer and consumer compatibility during real coexistence windows.

# Applicability

Apply this rule when producers and consumers can change independently, retained messages may be consumed later, external parties depend on the contract, or old and new contract forms may coexist.

Do not apply this rule when producer and consumer are changed atomically within one controlled scope and no retained or external messages require coexistence. Compatibility requirements are contextual, not universal.

# Rule Statement

Message contract changes must preserve the compatibility needed for the actual producer-consumer coexistence window, including old consumers with new producers and new consumers with old or retained messages when those combinations can occur.

# Rationale

Messaging decouples time and deployment, so producers and consumers often run different contract assumptions. Incompatible field removal, required-field addition, type change, semantic change, or strict deserialization can silently break consumers even when versioning exists.

# Evidence Required

Evaluation requires evidence of old and new contracts, producer behavior, consumer deserialization and interpretation, deployment sequence, retained messages, defaults, unknown-field handling, compatibility tests, transformations, and documentation.

Primary evidence includes schemas, payload definitions, consumers, serializer settings, tests using old and new messages, deployment records, migration notes, and failure logs. Version labels alone are not enough.

# Evaluation Guidance

Evaluate compatibility as a relationship outcome. Versioning may help identify contracts but does not prove compatibility. Schema evolution may describe field changes but does not prove that consumers tolerate them in the real rollout.

Common finding shapes include removing consumed fields, adding required fields that old producers cannot supply, changing type or units without consumer adaptation, changing meaning while keeping the same field, rejecting unknown fields, assuming coordinated rollout without evidence, and silent consumer breakage.

Remediation may include adding defaults, keeping old fields during migration, tolerating unknown fields, adding transformations, coordinating rollout, documenting compatibility windows, or retiring consumers before incompatible change. Do not require backward compatibility forever.

Boundaries: `MSG-002` owns boundary leakage, `MSG-007` owns version identity, and `MSG-009` owns structural and semantic schema evolution.

# Pass Conditions

Use `Pass` when evidence shows producer and consumer combinations that can occur are compatible or intentionally coordinated, with adequate handling of old, new, and retained message forms for the reviewed context.

# Fail Conditions

Use `Fail` when direct evidence shows contract changes that break consumers or producers during a real coexistence window, such as required-field, removal, type, semantic, or strict parsing changes with architectural impact.

# Warning Conditions

Use `Warning` when compatibility appears partial, untested, undocumented, dependent on assumptions, or fragile, but direct evidence of a confirmed break is incomplete.

# Not Applicable Conditions

Use `Not Applicable` when no independent coexistence window, retained message issue, contract change, or compatibility relationship exists in the reviewed scope.

# Not Enough Evidence Conditions

Use `Not Enough Evidence` when material does not show producer and consumer versions, deployment timing, old and new schemas, retained messages, or deserialization and interpretation behavior.

# Severity Guidance

Severity depends on affected consumers, independent deployment, external exposure, silent failure risk, data loss, business criticality, recovery cost, and detectability. A breaking change in a retired contract may be low or not applicable.

# Confidence Guidance

Use `Confirmed` when direct evidence shows compatible or incompatible behavior across actual producer-consumer combinations. Use `Likely` for multiple corroborating signals with incomplete rollout evidence. Use `Possible` for limited or indirect compatibility signals. Use `Not Enough Evidence` when coexistence cannot be evaluated. Version labels alone must not produce `Confirmed`.

# Dependencies

MSG-002, MSG-007, MSG-009.
