# Rule ID

MSG-003

# Title

Event ownership

# Category

Events and Messaging

# Status

Active

# Intent

Evaluate whether responsibility for defining, producing, operating, and evolving an event or message is identifiable enough to support reliable change.

# Applicability

Apply this rule when reviewed material includes an event or message produced for one or more consumers, retained for later use, published through a shared channel, or documented as a contract.

Do not apply this rule to isolated internal implementation callbacks, local method notifications, logs, or purely technical records with no message contract or consumer relationship. Ownership does not require a formal team structure and may be evidenced by module, bounded context, service, repository, CODEOWNERS, ADR, operational responsibility, or documentation.

# Rule Statement

Each event or message contract must have identifiable ownership for its definition, publication responsibility, operational accountability, and evolution authority when consumers or retained messages depend on it.

# Rationale

Messages without ownership are difficult to change safely. Consumers may not know who can clarify meaning, producers may publish incompatible variants, operational incidents may lack an accountable resolver, and contract evolution can become accidental.

Ownership is accountability, not a prescribed organization chart. The rule evaluates whether the reviewed material makes responsibility reviewable.

# Evidence Required

Evaluation requires evidence identifying the message, producer or producers, consumers or consumer governance, contract location, change authority, and operational responsibility where applicable.

Primary evidence includes producer code, service or module boundaries, contract repositories, schema ownership, CODEOWNERS, ADRs, deployment ownership, runbooks, incident ownership, consumer registrations, publishing permissions, documentation, and change records. Namespaces and folders are supporting signals only.

# Evaluation Guidance

Evaluate whether a reviewer can determine who owns the message definition, who is allowed to change it, who is responsible for publishing valid messages, who can coordinate affected consumers, and who handles operational correction when publication or consumption fails.

Common finding shapes include global event libraries with no responsible owner, multiple producers emitting incompatible meanings under one contract, shared ownership without coordination evidence, consumers depending on orphaned contracts, and operational incidents routed to no accountable component.

Remediation may include assigning contract ownership to a service, module, context, repository, or operational group; documenting change authority; limiting producers; separating incompatible contracts; or establishing a lightweight consumer notification path. Do not require a specific team model, governance tool, registry, or organization process.

Boundaries: `MSG-002` evaluates integration boundary quality, while this rule evaluates accountability. `MSG-007` evaluates versioning strategy, not who owns it. Compatibility, semantic correctness, retry, dead-letter, and traceability remain in their own rules.

# Pass Conditions

Use `Pass` when evidence shows identifiable ownership for message definition, publication responsibility, change authority, and operational accountability appropriate to the reviewed scope.

# Fail Conditions

Use `Fail` when direct evidence shows a consumed or retained message contract has no identifiable owner, conflicting producers with no coordinating authority, or unclear accountability that creates real change or operational risk.

# Warning Conditions

Use `Warning` when ownership exists only partially, is implied by location, is inconsistent across producers and consumers, or is documented but not corroborated enough for a definitive result.

# Not Applicable Conditions

Use `Not Applicable` when the reviewed scope contains no produced messages, no consumer relationship, no retained message contract, or only local technical notifications outside this responsibility.

# Not Enough Evidence Conditions

Use `Not Enough Evidence` when ownership may matter but reviewed material does not show producers, consumers, contract source, change authority, operational responsibility, or documentation sufficient to evaluate accountability.

# Severity Guidance

Severity increases with critical flows, many consumers, independent deployments, external parties, financial or regulatory impact, incident frequency, and difficulty of coordinating fixes. Severity may be lower for local or low-impact messages with narrow ownership ambiguity.

# Confidence Guidance

Use `Confirmed` when direct evidence identifies or disproves ownership across definition, publication, change, and operation. Use `Likely` when several signals align but one dimension is incomplete. Use `Possible` for weak or indirect ownership signals. Use `Not Enough Evidence` when accountability cannot be determined. Naming or repository location alone must not produce `Confirmed`.

# Dependencies

MSG-002, MSG-007. These are adjacent responsibilities, not procedural prerequisites.
