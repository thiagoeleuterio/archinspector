# Rule ID

MSG-007

# Title

Event contract versioning

# Category

Events and Messaging

# Status

Active

# Intent

Evaluate whether incompatible message contract changes or contract coexistence are controlled by an explicit, understandable versioning approach when the context requires it.

# Applicability

Apply this rule when messages are consumed across a boundary, retained over time, consumed by independently changing consumers, or documented as contracts that may change incompatibly.

Do not apply this rule to local messages with coordinated producer and consumer change, contracts that do not evolve in the reviewed scope, or situations where compatibility is managed without distinct versions and evidence shows that version identity is unnecessary.

# Rule Statement

Message contract versions must be identifiable and managed when incompatible changes, retained messages, or independent consumer timelines require consumers and operators to distinguish contract forms.

# Rationale

Versioning makes contract identity explicit during incompatible change and coexistence. Without it, consumers may deserialize or interpret the wrong contract, migrations become unclear, and old retained messages may be processed with assumptions meant for a newer shape.

The rule does not require a version number in every message or a specific strategy. Versioning may be represented by envelope, type, topic, schema identity, endpoint, contract package, documentation, or another explicit mechanism.

# Evidence Required

Evaluation requires evidence of contract identity, change history, producer and consumer versions, retained messages, migration strategy, version lifecycle, deployment timing, schemas, documentation, or tests.

Primary evidence includes schemas, envelope metadata, routing conventions tied to contract identity, contract repositories, migrations, consumer selection logic, deployment records, compatibility tests, and ADRs. Names alone are supporting signals.

# Evaluation Guidance

First determine whether versioning is required by the reviewed context. Independent deployment, retained messages, external consumers, incompatible changes, or contract coexistence increase applicability. Coordinated release may reduce or remove applicability.

Common finding shapes include incompatible contract change without distinguishable version, consumers unable to identify the contract they received, several versions with no lifecycle, version fields that are ignored, over-versioning that creates unnecessary contract sprawl, and implicit versioning not documented enough to operate.

Remediation may include documenting an explicit versioning approach, adding version identity through an existing envelope or channel, defining lifecycle and migration rules, coordinating retirement, or simplifying unnecessary versions. Do not prescribe semantic versioning, schema registry, or version field universally.

Boundaries: `MSG-003` owns ownership; `MSG-008` owns compatibility during coexistence; `MSG-009` owns schema evolution mechanics.

# Pass Conditions

Use `Pass` when evidence shows that required contract versions are identifiable, producers and consumers can distinguish them, coexistence or migration is understandable, and version lifecycle is adequate for the reviewed context.

# Fail Conditions

Use `Fail` when direct evidence shows incompatible changes, retained message differences, or active coexistence without a usable versioning approach, causing real risk to consumers or operations.

# Warning Conditions

Use `Warning` when versioning exists but is incomplete, undocumented, inconsistently used, excessive, or weakly connected to consumer behavior without enough evidence for `Fail`.

# Not Applicable Conditions

Use `Not Applicable` when messages are local, non-retained, coordinated in one deployment scope, not evolving incompatibly, or otherwise do not require distinguishable contract versions.

# Not Enough Evidence Conditions

Use `Not Enough Evidence` when the rule may apply but material does not show change history, consumer timelines, retained messages, version identifiers, migration approach, or contract usage.

# Severity Guidance

Severity depends on consumer independence, external exposure, retained data, incompatible change impact, operational recovery, regulatory or financial risk, and cost of migration. Do not map missing version fields automatically to high severity.

# Confidence Guidance

Use `Confirmed` when direct evidence shows versioning need and versioning behavior or absence. Use `Likely` when multiple sources support the conclusion but lifecycle or consumers are partly unknown. Use `Possible` for limited signals. Use `Not Enough Evidence` when versioning need cannot be established. Naming alone must not produce `Confirmed`.
