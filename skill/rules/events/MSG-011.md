# Rule ID

MSG-011

# Title

Transactional publication record

# Category

Events and Messaging

# Status

Active

# Intent

Evaluate whether a durable transactional publication record or equivalent capture mechanism supports reliable recovery, controlled resend, and publication state tracking when adopted or required.

# Applicability

Apply this rule when the architecture uses, claims, or requires a durable record of publication intent, an outbox-like mechanism, change capture, transaction log, broker transaction, or equivalent mechanism to satisfy producer-persistence consistency.

Do not apply this rule merely because `MSG-010` is relevant. Absence of an outbox-like record is not a failure when another strategy satisfies the consistency need or when recoverable publication is not required.

# Rule Statement

When a durable transactional publication record is adopted or required, it must capture publication intent reliably, preserve enough state for recovery, support controlled resend, and make publication progress or failure observable.

# Rationale

A publication record that is not transactional, durable, selectable, recoverable, or observable may create false confidence. Messages can remain stuck, be sent repeatedly without control, disappear after a crash, or be cleaned up before recovery.

# Evidence Required

Evaluation requires evidence of record creation, transaction relationship, durability, message payload or reference, status fields, selection and locking, dispatcher behavior, retry and resend control, cleanup, retention, recovery tests, monitoring, and operational procedures.

Primary evidence includes outbox or equivalent tables/documents/logs, transaction boundaries, dispatcher code, concurrency controls, status transitions, failure handling, tests, metrics, logs, and ADRs. The mechanism need not be relational or named Outbox.

# Evaluation Guidance

Evaluate the adopted mechanism's reliability, not the universal need for the pattern. Confirm whether publication intent is captured atomically with relevant state, whether unsent records can be found, whether concurrent dispatchers avoid uncontrolled duplication, whether stuck records are detectable, and whether cleanup preserves required recovery.

Common finding shapes include intent recorded outside the transaction, records without status, dispatcher crashes leaving messages invisible, unsafe concurrent selection, no retry or stuck-message path, premature cleanup, and records that omit payload or routing data needed to publish.

Remediation may include moving capture into the relevant transaction, adding status and timestamps, making selection concurrency-safe, preserving retry context, adding recovery queries, defining retention, or documenting equivalent capture behavior. Do not require a specific table, broker, or library.

Boundaries: `MSG-010` evaluates consistency need; this rule evaluates the durable record solution. `MSG-014` evaluates duplicate recognition and handling after resend. `MSG-016` evaluates retry policy; duplicate and delivery rules remain separate.

# Pass Conditions

Use `Pass` when evidence shows the publication record or equivalent mechanism is durable, transactionally aligned when required, recoverable, observable, and capable of controlled resend for the reviewed context.

# Fail Conditions

Use `Fail` when direct evidence shows the adopted or required record cannot reliably capture intent, recover unsent messages, track state, handle dispatcher failure, or prevent uncontrolled resend in a way that creates architectural risk.

# Warning Conditions

Use `Warning` when the mechanism exists but is incomplete, weakly observable, partially transactional, operationally fragile, or not fully evidenced for all relevant failure paths.

# Not Applicable Conditions

Use `Not Applicable` when no durable publication record is adopted or required, another strategy satisfies producer consistency, or publication recovery is outside the reviewed scope.

# Not Enough Evidence Conditions

Use `Not Enough Evidence` when material does not show record creation, transaction boundaries, dispatcher behavior, durability, status, recovery, cleanup, or operational handling.

# Severity Guidance

Severity depends on message criticality, publication loss risk, duplicate impact, stuck-message detectability, consumer impact, recovery cost, and operational exposure. Do not assign severity from absence of a table name.

# Confidence Guidance

Use `Confirmed` when direct evidence shows the record mechanism and its recovery behavior or defect. Use `Likely` when several signals align but some operational detail is missing. Use `Possible` for limited or indirect evidence. Use `Not Enough Evidence` when mechanism behavior cannot be inspected. Naming alone must not produce `Confirmed`.

# Dependencies

MSG-010, MSG-014, MSG-016. These are adjacent responsibilities, not procedural prerequisites.
