# Rule ID

MSG-014

# Title

Duplicate message handling

# Category

Events and Messaging

# Status

Active

# Intent

Evaluate whether expected, possible, or observed duplicate messages are recognized, tolerated, detected, discarded, merged, or otherwise handled according to flow impact.

# Applicability

Apply this rule when transport behavior, retries, replay, multiple producers, uncertain acknowledgements, publication recovery, or business processes can produce duplicate transport messages or semantically duplicate messages.

Do not apply this rule when duplicate messages cannot occur in the reviewed context or when duplicates are legitimate independent business facts.

# Rule Statement

The architecture must recognize and handle duplicate messages when duplicates can occur and matter, distinguishing harmful duplicates from legitimate repeated business facts.

# Rationale

Duplicates can arise from transport redelivery, producer retry, dispatcher recovery, multiple producers, replay, or business-level repetition. Even when consumers are idempotent, the architecture may still need to recognize duplicates for diagnostics, routing, merge, discard, or operational control.

# Evidence Required

Evaluation requires evidence of duplicate sources, message identifiers, business keys, producer retry, dispatcher resend, consumer duplicate checks, logs, tests, merge or discard behavior, and documentation.

Primary evidence includes producer and consumer code, processed-message records, business uniqueness constraints, duplicate handling branches, retry/replay paths, operational logs, metrics, and integration tests.

# Evaluation Guidance

Evaluate duplicate existence and handling, not only effect idempotency. Determine whether duplicates are expected, possible, or observed; whether they are transport duplicates or semantic duplicates; and whether the response is discard, merge, tolerate, route, alert, or process as a legitimate new fact.

Common finding shapes include ignoring duplicate redelivery despite at-least-once transport, treating all repeated business keys as errors, no detection for duplicate publication from recovery, duplicate logs without handling path, and dedupe by transport ID when business duplicates arrive under new IDs.

Remediation may include identifying duplicate sources, selecting message or business keys, documenting legitimate duplicates, adding discard or merge behavior, tolerating harmless duplicates, improving diagnostics, or coordinating with idempotency. Do not require universal deduplication.

Boundaries: `MSG-013` evaluates repeated effect safety; this rule evaluates duplicate recognition and handling. `MSG-012` evaluates delivery semantics; `MSG-015` evaluates ordering interaction; `MSG-020` evaluates replay.

# Pass Conditions

Use `Pass` when evidence shows duplicates that can occur are appropriately recognized, tolerated, detected, discarded, merged, routed, or intentionally treated as legitimate for the reviewed impact.

# Fail Conditions

Use `Fail` when direct evidence shows harmful duplicates are possible or observed and the architecture lacks recognition or handling, causing real duplicate outcomes or operational risk.

# Warning Conditions

Use `Warning` when duplicate handling is partial, inconsistent, untested, limited to one duplicate source, or unable to distinguish transport duplicates from legitimate repeats.

# Not Applicable Conditions

Use `Not Applicable` when duplicates cannot occur or do not matter in the reviewed scope, or when repeated messages represent legitimate independent facts.

# Not Enough Evidence Conditions

Use `Not Enough Evidence` when material does not show duplicate sources, delivery behavior, producer retry, consumer handling, identifiers, business keys, or operational evidence.

# Severity Guidance

Severity depends on duplicate business impact, side effects, consumer count, observability, recovery cost, regulatory or financial exposure, and whether duplicates can cascade or block processing.

# Confidence Guidance

Use `Confirmed` when direct evidence shows duplicate sources and handling or absence. Use `Likely` when multiple signals support the conclusion but some source or consumer detail is missing. Use `Possible` for limited signals. Use `Not Enough Evidence` when duplicate behavior cannot be evaluated. Identifier presence alone must not produce `Confirmed`.

MSG-012, MSG-013, MSG-015, MSG-020.
